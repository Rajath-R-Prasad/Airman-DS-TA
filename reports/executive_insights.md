## 1. Overview

This report presents the key strategic insights derived from a comprehensive analysis of Airman Aeronautics' two core platforms:

- **Skynet SaaS** — Flight dispatch, scheduling, and operations management
- **TOGA App** — Cadet study tracking, quiz performance, and learning analytics

The assessment covered **200 cadets**, **30 instructors**, **25 aircraft**, and **12,001 sortie records** across a 6-month operational window.

## 3. Top 5 Strategic Insights

### Insight 1: Dispatch Reliability Is Strong but Delay Management Needs Attention
With a ~90% sortie completion rate, Skynet demonstrates strong dispatch reliability. However, the average delay of ~26 minutes per completed sortie suggests systemic scheduling inefficiencies. Approximately 30% of flights experience delays exceeding 60 minutes, indicating a need for improved slot management and buffer optimization.

### Insight 2: Aircraft Utilization Is Critically Low
Average fleet utilization sits at just ~2.9%, meaning the majority of available aircraft hours go unused. This represents a significant cost inefficiency. Recommendations include fleet right-sizing, dynamic scheduling optimization, and exploring revenue-sharing partnerships with other flight schools during off-peak hours.

### Insight 3: Weather Is the Leading Cancellation Driver
Weather-related cancellations account for the largest share of cancelled sorties, followed by Aircraft Defects and ATC Restrictions. Investment in advanced weather forecasting integration within Skynet could reduce weather-driven cancellations by enabling proactive rescheduling.

### Insight 4: Study-Flight Misalignment Creates Hidden Risk
The analysis reveals that many cadets with strong flight progress have significantly lagging study performance (and vice versa). This misalignment — visible in the Flight vs. Study Progress scatter plot — suggests that training curricula are not sufficiently synchronized. Cadets in the "Flight Ahead of Study" quadrant may be progressing through practical training without adequate theoretical grounding, creating potential safety and examination risks.

### Insight 5: Payment Collection Requires Intervention
With ~28% of invoiced amounts remaining outstanding, Airman faces a tangible cash flow risk. Ten cadets have been flagged with payment data discrepancies. Implementing automated payment reminders, early-warning triggers for overdue accounts, and payment plan options could materially improve collection efficiency.

## 4. Risk Profile Summary

The weighted risk scoring model (0–100 scale) evaluates each cadet across five dimensions:
- Training pace (flight progress) — 25%
- Study coverage — 20%
- Knowledge quality (quiz scores) — 15%
- Study activity (days since last session) — 20%
- Financial health (payment status) — 20%

**Distribution:**
- **Low Risk (0–40):** ~35% of cadets — on track across all dimensions
- **Medium Risk (40–70):** ~65% of cadets — require monitoring and targeted intervention
- **High Risk (70–100):** ~0% of cadets — no cadets currently in critical risk territory

The most common risk drivers across the cohort are:
1. Low flight progress
2. Study inactivity
3. Low study coverage

---

## 6. Conclusion

Airman Aeronautics operates a fundamentally sound training program with strong dispatch reliability. However, the data reveals clear opportunities for improvement in aircraft utilization, delay management, study-flight alignment, and payment collection. The integrated risk scoring model provides a powerful tool for proactive cadet management and should be operationalized within the Skynet/TOGA ecosystem.
